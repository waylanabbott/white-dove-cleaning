Spring Cleaning Checklist for Indianapolis Homeowners

May 19, 2026 by White Dove Cleaning

Indiana winters are tough on homes. Months of road salt tracked across your floors, windows sealed shut against the cold, and heating systems pushing dry, dusty air through every room all take a toll. When spring finally arrives in Indianapolis, your home needs more than a quick tidy-up. It needs a thorough spring cleaning.

This room-by-room checklist will help you tackle the post-winter buildup and get your home ready for the warmer months ahead. Whether you plan to do it yourself or bring in a professional cleaning team like White Dove Cleaning Services, this guide covers everything that needs attention.

Kitchen

The kitchen is the heart of the home and usually the area that accumulates the most hidden grime over winter. Start here and work your way through the rest of the house.

  • Pull out the refrigerator and stove to clean behind and underneath them
  • Degrease the range hood, filter, and exhaust fan
  • Deep clean the oven interior, including racks
  • Wipe down all cabinet fronts and handles
  • Sanitize the inside of the microwave, dishwasher, and trash can
  • Clean and organize the pantry, checking for expired items
  • Scrub tile grout and reseal if needed
  • Wash light fixtures and replace any burned-out bulbs

Bathrooms

Bathrooms collect moisture, soap scum, and mildew year-round, but winter makes it worse because reduced ventilation lets humidity build up. Give every bathroom a thorough treatment.

  • Scrub tile grout with a mildew-fighting cleaner
  • Remove hard water stains from showerheads, faucets, and glass doors
  • Clean and disinfect the toilet inside and out, including the base
  • Wash or replace the shower curtain and liner
  • Wipe down the exhaust fan and vent cover
  • Clean out medicine cabinets and under-sink storage
  • Launder bath mats, towels, and any fabric accessories

Bedrooms

After months of sleeping with windows closed and the furnace running, bedrooms collect an impressive amount of dust. This is especially important for allergy sufferers, since spring pollen is about to make things worse.

  • Wash all bedding, including comforters, mattress pads, and pillow protectors
  • Vacuum the mattress and flip or rotate it
  • Dust all surfaces, including the tops of dressers, nightstands, and headboards
  • Clean under the bed and behind furniture
  • Wipe down ceiling fans and light fixtures
  • Vacuum or clean window treatments (curtains, blinds, shades)
  • Go through closets and donate clothes you no longer wear

Living Areas

Living rooms, family rooms, and dens are high-traffic areas that need extra attention during spring cleaning. These rooms tend to accumulate dust in upholstery and pet hair in carpets throughout the winter.

  • Vacuum upholstered furniture, including under cushions
  • Deep clean carpets or schedule a professional carpet cleaning
  • Dust bookshelves, entertainment centers, and decorative items
  • Clean baseboards and door frames throughout the home
  • Wipe down all light switches, outlet covers, and door handles
  • Wash interior windows and window sills
  • Move furniture and vacuum or mop underneath

Indianapolis-Specific Spring Cleaning Tips

Living in central Indiana means dealing with seasonal challenges that homeowners in other parts of the country may not face. Here are some spring cleaning tasks that are especially relevant for Indianapolis and surrounding communities like Carmel, Fishers, and Greenwood.

Tackle Road Salt Residue

Indiana roads get heavy salt treatments all winter, and that salt gets tracked into your home on shoes, boots, and pet paws. By spring, you will find white residue along entryways, on hard floors, and embedded in carpet fibers near doors. Mop hard floors with a vinegar-and-water solution to dissolve salt buildup, and deep clean carpets in entryway areas to remove ground-in residue.

Prepare for Pollen Season

Indianapolis typically sees its peak pollen season from April through June. Tree pollen comes first, followed by grass pollen that can linger well into summer. Before you start opening windows to enjoy the spring air, make sure your home is as clean as possible to minimize the pollen that accumulates indoors.

  • Replace HVAC filters with high-efficiency options rated MERV 11 or higher
  • Clean air vents and return registers throughout the home
  • Set up a shoe-removal station at your main entry to keep pollen from spreading
  • Dust and vacuum frequently during peak pollen weeks

Post-Winter HVAC Check

Your furnace has been running hard for months, and the ductwork has been circulating dust and allergens throughout your home. Spring is the ideal time to clean your air vents, replace filters, and schedule a professional HVAC inspection before you switch over to air conditioning. Clean ducts mean cleaner air and better system efficiency.

Storm Season Preparation

Indiana's severe weather season typically runs from March through June. While spring cleaning is primarily about the inside of your home, it is also a good time to check that your home is ready for storms. Clear gutters of winter debris, inspect your basement or crawl space for moisture issues, and make sure your emergency supplies are accessible and up to date.
