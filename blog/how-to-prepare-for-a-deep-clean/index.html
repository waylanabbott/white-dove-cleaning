How to Prepare Your Home for a Deep Clean

May 18, 2025 by White Dove Cleaning

Booking a professional deep cleaning is one of the best things you can do for your home. Whether you are scheduling your first appointment with a cleaning service in Indianapolis or you have been a loyal client for years, a little preparation before your cleaning team arrives can make a big difference in the results you see.

At White Dove Cleaning Services, we want every client to get the absolute most out of their deep cleaning session. Here is our guide to getting your home ready so our team can focus on the heavy-duty work that truly transforms your space.

Declutter Surfaces and Floors

The single most impactful thing you can do before a deep clean is to pick up clutter. We are not talking about a full home reorganization. Simply clearing countertops, tables, nightstands, and floors of personal items, toys, mail, and miscellaneous belongings allows our cleaners to access every surface.

When surfaces are covered with items, your cleaning team has to work around them, which means less time spent on actual scrubbing, sanitizing, and polishing. A quick ten-minute sweep through each room to put things in their proper places makes a noticeable difference.

  • Put away dishes and clear kitchen counters
  • Pick up clothes, shoes, and accessories from floors and furniture
  • Move toys to a bin or closet
  • Clear bathroom counters of toiletries you can temporarily store in a cabinet

Secure Valuables and Sensitive Items

Before your cleaning appointment, take a few minutes to put away anything fragile, irreplaceable, or sensitive. This includes jewelry, important documents, cash, heirloom items, and anything you would rather not have moved during the cleaning process.

Professional cleaners are trustworthy and careful, but accidents can happen when items are in high-traffic work areas. Putting these things in a drawer or safe gives you peace of mind and lets our team work confidently without worrying about damaging something precious.

Make a Plan for Pets

We love animals, and many of our clients in Carmel, Fishers, and across the Indianapolis metro have pets at home. However, deep cleaning involves opening cabinets, moving furniture, and using cleaning products that can be stressful or unsafe for curious pets.

If possible, arrange for your pets to be in a separate room, a crate, or outside during the cleaning. If your dog is anxious around strangers, let us know ahead of time. A heads-up about pets also helps our team plan for any pet hair removal, which is one of the most common deep cleaning requests we receive.

Communicate Your Priorities

Every home is different, and every homeowner has areas that matter most to them. Maybe your kitchen has not had a thorough scrub-down in months. Maybe the bathrooms need extra attention. Perhaps you want us to focus on baseboards, ceiling fans, or the inside of your oven.

Before your appointment, make a short list of your top priorities and share them with your cleaning team. At White Dove Cleaning Services, we customize every deep clean to match your needs, but knowing your priorities upfront helps us allocate time effectively and deliver results that exceed your expectations.

Provide Access and Parking

This one is simple but easy to overlook. Make sure your cleaning team can get into your home easily. If you will not be present during the cleaning, arrange for a key, a lockbox code, or a garage code. Let us know about any alarm systems we should be aware of.

If you live in an apartment or condo complex, provide any gate codes or visitor parking instructions. The smoother the arrival process, the more time we can spend on cleaning rather than logistics.

What to Expect During a Deep Clean

A deep clean goes far beyond a standard maintenance cleaning. When our team arrives at your Indianapolis home, you can expect us to tackle areas that do not get attention during routine cleanings:

  • Scrubbing tile grout and removing soap scum buildup in bathrooms
  • Degreasing and sanitizing kitchen appliances inside and out
  • Cleaning behind and under furniture
  • Wiping down baseboards, door frames, and light switches
  • Dusting ceiling fans, light fixtures, and vents
  • Deep vacuuming carpets and mopping hard floors
  • Cleaning interior windows and window sills

A typical deep clean takes longer than a standard cleaning session. Depending on the size of your home and the level of buildup, expect the process to take anywhere from three to six hours. We encourage clients to plan accordingly and, if possible, be out of the house during the clean so our team can work without interruption.

After the Deep Clean

Once your deep clean is complete, maintaining your home becomes much easier. Many of our clients in Westfield, Noblesville, and the surrounding areas schedule recurring maintenance cleanings after their initial deep clean. This keeps their home in top shape without the need for another intensive session for months.

A deep clean resets the baseline. Regular upkeep preserves it. That is the approach we recommend for every household we serve across the Indianapolis metro area.
